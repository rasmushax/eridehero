Speed & Motor Performance

The Ninebot Max G2 is powered by a 450W nominal motor (900W peak) that delivers solid, consistent performance. While it won't win any drag races, the power delivery is smooth and predictable â€“ exactly what you want for a commuter scooter.

In our testing, the Max G2 achieved a true top speed of 22.1 mph on flat ground, right in line with Segway's claims. Acceleration from 0-15 mph takes about 4.2 seconds, which is adequate for city riding.

Ride Quality

The Max G2 offers a comfortable, stable ride thanks to its 10-inch tubeless pneumatic tires and front suspension fork. The wide deck provides ample foot room, and the overall geometry feels planted and confidence-inspiring at speed.

Braking

Stopping power is excellent, with a front drum brake and rear electronic brake working in tandem. From 15 mph, we measured stopping distances of just 12.4 feet â€“ among the best in class.

Build Quality & Features

Segway's build quality is second to none. The Max G2 feels like it's built to last, with solid materials and tight tolerances throughout. Notable features include:
